An AI grill that doesn’t ask for salt.

January 21, 2026 • 2 min read • Category: grilling tech

If you’ve ever stood in front of a grill wondering whether this side should be flipped yet, don’t worry — someone has apparently solved that for you. Meet the Perfecta from SEERGRILLS, a United Kingdom-based startup promising an AI-powered grilling experience so “perfect” it theoretically eliminates guesswork entirely. It’s being touted as the world’s first grill that uses machine intelligence to monitor your food and adjust heat, time, and sear levels automatically so that your food hits your preferred doneness without you babysitting the flames.

Under the hood is SEERGRILLS’ proprietary NeuralFire® technology: a combo of sensors, dual vertical infrared burners, and machine learning that watches temperature, thickness, and other environmental variables in real time. You tell it whether you want rare or well done via the built-in touchscreen or companion app, and the Perfecta goes to work — no flipping, no guesswork, and almost no human intervention. Its vertical infrared system cooks all sides at once at temperatures up to 1,652°F, which is apparently hot enough to turn meat into dinner in under three minutes.

The product isn’t cheap — base units and bundles (including extra accessories like VertiGrates and prep stations) run into the high thousands — but for the person who takes grilling seriously, that’s sort of the point: leave the sweating over a grill to everyone else and let the AI get on with it. Whether this is the future of backyard BBQs or just one more way to make an appliance slightly more complicated than it needs to be, well, that’s up to you. At the very least, it’s a conversation starter for the next cookout.
