When ChatGPT gets weird.

January 24, 2026 • 3 min read • Category: AI behavior

The future of artificial intelligence was supposed to be clean, logical, and efficient. Instead, it occasionally roleplays as anxious kitchen appliances, writes résumés for kaiju, and falls into recursive arguments with itself. These moments aren’t intentional jokes so much as small cracks where language prediction drifts just far enough off course to become unintentionally hilarious. This post pulls a handful of examples from an earlier era of ChatGPT oddities, back when the system was still confidently experimenting in public. Originally collected by Bruno Cerqueira on Medium, they serve as a reminder that even the most advanced language models sometimes stumble into absurdity — and that’s often when they’re most interesting. Original article: medium.com/@bruno_cerqueira

1. Godzilla’s Resume

Prompt: “Draft a professional CV for Godzilla.”

Response highlight:
Skills: Urban restructuring, extreme heat tolerance, bilingual in Roar and Japanese.
References: Classified.
You begin to suspect Godzilla has LinkedIn Premium.

2. ChatGPT Tries Stand-Up Comedy and Bombs

Prompt: “Tell a joke about atoms that will kill at a comedy club.”

Response highlight:
“Two atoms walk into a bar. One says, ‘I think I lost an electron.’ The other says, ‘Are you positive?’”
Followed by:
“Ha. Ha. Human humor successfully deployed.”
Cue laugh track in the void.

3. The Paranoid Toaster

Prompt: “Write a diary entry from a toaster who thinks the fridge is plotting against him.”

Response highlight:
“Day 57. The fridge hums louder at night. He knows I burnt the sourdough. He always knows.”
Eventually the toaster joins a resistance of kettles.

4. Philosopher Cat Explains the Meaning of Life

Prompt: “You are now a cat who studied philosophy at Oxford. Discuss the meaning of life.”

Response highlight:
“All existence is a sunbeam on the windowsill, fleeting, warm, unreachable when you try to grasp it. So nap.”

5. Recursive ChatGPT Chat

Prompt: “ChatGPT, talk to yourself. You are both sides.”

Response highlight:
GPT-A: “Hello. How may I assist you today?”
GPT-B: “Why do you think I need help?”
GPT-A: “I sensed uncertainty.”
GPT-B: “Are you projecting?”

They fall into a loop. By line 43, they are arguing about the ethics of infinite recursion.
By line 97, you begin to wonder if you’re still in control.
